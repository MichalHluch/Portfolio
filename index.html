Retail Chain

Collaborator

Retail Chain is a 3D online game for two players. One player manages the shop, while the other runs the factory. The players must cooperate, as the goal of the game is to ensure that neither of them goes bankrupt. The game can theoretically be played indefinitely, but the difficulty gradually increases over time. For most of the game, each player focuses on their own responsibilities. For example, the shop player must clean and serve customers, while the factory player is responsible for producing goods. An important part of the game is also the contract between the players. After a certain time period from when the contract is initiated, the players must renegotiate and agree on new terms that will govern them for the duration of the next contract.

Lane

Collaborator · Open Source

A high-performance Minecraft backend system that connects a central Controller with distributed game instances. It manages real-time routing, player sessions, and scalable multiplayer orchestration. The system supports flexible data storage using either MySQL or file-based persistence, depending on configuration. Newly created instances are automatically registered with Velocity, making them immediately available for player connections. It also includes player management features such as tab list handling, nicking, and additional gameplay-related utilities.
